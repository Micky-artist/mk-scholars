The German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD) is offering scholarships to support Master's and PhD studies for individuals in Sub-Saharan Africa. This initiative aims to strengthen higher education systems in the region, contributing to sustainable development. The scholarships are intended for those aspiring to pursue careers in teaching and research at higher education institutions within Sub-Saharan Africa.

The program primarily targets university staff but also addresses the broader need for academically trained professionals in the public sector. Female candidates and applicants from less privileged regions or groups are especially encouraged to apply.

SCHOLARSHIP BENEFITS
Recipients of the DAAD scholarship will receive:

• Monthly Stipend: Financial support to cover living expenses.
• Study and Research Allowance: Funds to assist with academic materials and research activities.
• Thesis Publishing Costs: Support for the publication of research findings.
• Tuition Fees: Coverage of tuition costs for the duration of the program.

For In-Region scholarship holders (those studying in a country other than their home country), additional benefits include:
• Travel Allowance: Financial support for travel expenses to and from the host institution.
• Health Insurance: Coverage for medical expenses during the study period.

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
To be eligible for the scholarship, applicants must:

• Educational Background: Hold a first academic degree (e.g., Bachelor's) when applying for a Master's program or a Master's degree when applying for a PhD program.
• Nationality: Be a graduate or postgraduate from a Sub-Saharan African country.
• Study Location: Intend to pursue studies either in their home country (In-Country scholarships) or in another Sub-Saharan African country (In-Region scholarships).

2. Namibia:

REQUIRED DOCUMENTS
Applicants are typically required to submit the following documents:

• Completed Application Form: Available on the DAAD portal.
• Curriculum Vitae (CV): A detailed CV outlining academic and professional experiences.
• Academic Transcripts and Certificates: Certified copies of previous academic records.
• Research Proposal or Study Plan: Depending on the program, a detailed outline of the proposed research or study objectives.
• Letter of Motivation: A personal statement explaining the applicant's motivation and how the program aligns with their career goals.
• Recommendation Letters: These are Usually from academic or professional referees attesting to the applicant's qualifications and suitability for the program.

Specific document requirements may vary by institution and program. Applicants should consult the respective calls for applications for detailed information.

APPLICATION PROCEDURES
The application process involves the following steps:

1. Identify the Desired Program: Review the list of available programs and select the one that aligns with your academic and career aspirations.
2. Prepare Application Documents: Gather all required documents as specified in the call for applications.
3. Submit Application: Applications should be submitted directly to the respective host institution. Detailed submission guidelines are provided in the individual calls for applications.
4. Selection Process: The selection procedure varies between programs. Shortlisted candidates may be contacted for interviews or additional assessments.
Applicants are advised to carefully read the specific application instructions for each host institution, as procedures and requirements may differ.

ADDITIONAL INFORMATION

• South African Applicants: In-Country scholarships for students from South Africa are administered separately through a cooperation between DAAD and the National Research Foundation (NRF) with distinct application calls and deadlines.

• Encouragement for Underrepresented Groups: Female applicants and candidates from less privileged regions or groups are strongly encouraged to apply to promote diversity and inclusion within the academic community.

APPLICATION DEADLINE
The deadline for applications is April 9, 2025. Applicants should ensure that all materials are submitted by this date to be considered for the scholarship.
